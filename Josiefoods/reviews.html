LA edition: Mango Coconut Snow from DTF

This was a very interesting dish that I was intrigued to try just from the name. I was expecting some kind of icecream but what I got was shaved ice/cream (not fully icecream) that was coconut flavored and had mango bits on top. The mango bits were the best part but when you got a good bite it was overall pretty good. The ice/cream by itself was not very good though... 7.8/10.

LA edition: Chocolate Dumplings from DTF

These were absolutely fantastic, I highly reccomend. Not only was the chocolate sauce incredible but the combination of the outer layer of the dumpling and the sauce was very satisfying. The only thing about this dish I did not enjoyed was the salted cream sauce on the side but it was avoidable. Overall 8.6/10.

LA edition: Chicken Soup Dumplings from DTF

I really did not like these but they are a customer favorite so maybe I was missing something. The taste of the "soup" inside the dumpling was metallic and reminded me of the taste of chicken gone bad. Everyone else at the table enjoyed them but I will never order this again. 0/10.

LA edition: Garlic Green Beans from DTF

LA edition: Spicy Chicken Wontons from DTF

These were the best dumpling type of dish I had at DTF and I would also order these again. These also had a kick, which in this case made me want to go back for more. I really loved the shape of the wonton and the outside part was very nice. 8.9/10

LA edition: Spicy Peanut Noodles from DTF

Wow these were good I am reminising. I very much enjoyed the peanut flavor and they were actually quite spciy but that gave it a nice kick. You had to first mix on/in the sauce, which is what really made the noodles taste exquisite. 9.1/10.

LA edition: Ribs from DTF

LA edition: Hailey Bieber Smoothie and Coconut Cloud Smoothie from Erewhon

If I thought the buffalo cauliflour exceeded my expectations, these two beautiful drinks truly exceeded my expectations. While the Bieber smoothie was a little too sweet for my liking it was very tasty and is reminiscent of a childhood strawberry smoothie except 10x healthier. Both smoothies are a beauty to look at and now I understand the raive over the Erewhon smoothies. What really blew me away was the Coconut Cloud Smoothie. With the blue spirulina to give the smoothie its vibrant color, it looked like and tasted like the ocean.

LA edition: Chef's Special Salad, Buffalo Cauliflour, and Pasta Bolognese from Erewhon

I enjoyed this combo plate much more than I enjoyed the Sushi Sandwhich. The salad was probably the worst dish since it was dry and the ingredients did not seem to go well with eachother so when you put a bite into your mouth it felt like each flavor was fighting with eachother. The buffalo cauliflower exceeded my expectations and truly was a delight each bite. This is a create alternative to buffalo chicken and I would highly reccomend this dish. The pasta bolognese was the best part and I almost ate all of it. Normally I don't like bolognese but each bite was thick and very filling but also made you want to come back for more. Salad: 4/10 BC: 9/10 Bolognese: 9.7/10

LA edition: Spicy Sushi Sandwich from Erewhon

Did you know that "Erewhon" spelled backwards is nowhere? I think it is supposed to be for "La or nowhere" or something like that. Anyway, I was very interested in this sushi sandwhich from erewhon that I have seen all over my TikTok for you page so I had to give it a try. The first bite was very good as it had the most soy sauce on it but the more and more bites I had without the same amount of soy sauce it got worse and worse. But, the avocado and the salmon and I think tuna in the sandwhich were very high quality. But, the crispy rice could have been crispier because it needed more of a crunch. This could have just been because it was not very recently made. Overall I give this unique sandwhich a 7.4/10.

LA edition: Gnocchi and Brussel Sprouts at LA Prime

Margarita Pizza and Caeser Salad From CPK

The caeser salad alone is very thick and heavy but when you put it on top of each bite of your maragrita pizza, it's divine. 9.8/10

The Farmer Stand from Amnity Cafe

Vodka Pasta from the Hampton Social

While the truffle fries and caeser salad were amazing, the vodka pasta was disappointing. I was excited to get this dish and split it with my brother because I have had it in the past and it has been great. But, this time the sauce was watery and literally liquid when it should be thick and creamy. The pasta was barely coated in the sauce which made it taste like I was just eating pasta with red sauce. When I got a good bite it was an 8/10 but otherwise as a whole this was a 6/10. Sadness.

Caeser Salad with Chicken from the Hampton Social

The Hampton Social is really hitting it off with their dishes because I have no notes for this caeser salad. The chicken was perfectly grilled and easy to cut. What really makes a caeser great and not just good is when there is extra Parmesan on top because that is what the people want to see. 9.8/10.

Truffle fries from the Hampton Social

The truffle fries from The Hampton Social are the best truffle fries I have ever had. The amount of Parmesan on the fries and the aioli on the side are what dictate how good the fries are going to be. There was plenty Parmesan on top of the fries and then when you started to get deeper into the bowl, there was even more Parmesan on each fry underneath. The fries were crisp, salty and small so it did not feel like you were putting much into your mouth. The only downside of eating almost a whole bowl of truffle fries is that you are eating straight potato and need to go on a walk after dinner, which is exactly what I did. Overall, a 9.7/10.

Spicy Tuna Crispy Rice from Sushi San

The love of my life is spicy tuna crispy rice. Before trying this for the first time I went in knowing that there is an abundance of hype around the spicy tuna crispy rice from sushi San. So, my expectations were high and they did not disappoint in the slightest bit. The crispy rice is a perfect texture and always well cooked even if a little burnt. The tuna on top, mixed with spicy mayo, truffle pearl onions, sesame seeds and green onion, is a perfect level of spice. The texture is also great, the tuna kind of melts in your mouth. The sauce on top is impeccable and after you have your first one, which I usually do in 2-3 bites, I always come back wanting more. Consistently a 10/10.

Kabayaki eel from Sushi San

The kabayaki eel is my new favorite roll to get at sushi San. Being that I had never tried eel before I was scared to put one in my mouth. But, when Mia told me that it was really good after she had taken a bite, I knew that I had to try it since I trust Mia’s taste buds. It was great. The roll had a crunch to it coming from the eel and it was perfectly salty. I had to not think too much about how I was eating eel but otherwise the experience was wonderful. 9.8/10

Iced Matcha Latte from Soloway Coffee

At first I was skeptical because I forgot to order my matcha with vanilla, as I usually do. So, I expected a grass-tasting matcha like some of the other matchas I have tried in the past. But, when I took my first sip I realized that my expectations were way off; the matcha was perfect. The oatmilk tasted just right and the matcha truly tasted like how matcha is supposed to taste like: fresh, green, and delictable. I give this matcha a sold 10/10 with no notes.

Gravlax Bagel on an everything from Cupitol

The gravlax bagel is always a hit everytime I visit cupitol. The everything bagel is perfectly toasted and has the correct ratio of seeds: sesame, poppy, onion flakes and garlic flakes. I do wish there was more herb cream cheese, which I always find myself scraping the plate to get more of. But, the house-cured salmon seriously makes up for the lack of cream cheese with its delicious taste. Overall I would give this bagel a 9.6.
