Florence Nightingale

Florence Nightingale was the founder of modern nursing and she made hospitals a cleaner and safer place to be. She was born May 12, 1820 and died August 13, 1910. Her birthday is now International Nurses Day. Nightingale also trained nurses during the Crimean War and cared for wounded soldiers.
